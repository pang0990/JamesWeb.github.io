No one insults a goddess and remains unpunished. Tired and parched, Latona, the mother of Apollo and Diana, halted to quench her thirst at a pond. Some peasants working nearby haughtily prevent her from doing so. Latona, in a rage, turns them by throwing them into frogs and condemning them to spend eternity in the mire.

LATONA AND THE LYCIAN PEASANTS

JAN BRUEGHEL
